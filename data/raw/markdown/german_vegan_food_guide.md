# German Vegan Food Guide

This sample document is designed for a multilingual RAG pipeline. It contains short knowledge snippets about German dishes and vegan adaptations.

## Typical German dishes

Typical German dishes include Kartoffelsalat, Sauerkraut, Bratwurst, Schnitzel, Spätzle, Sauerbraten, Eintopf, Brezeln, and regional potato-based meals.

Many traditional German dishes contain meat, dairy, or eggs. However, several recipes can be adapted into vegan versions by replacing animal ingredients with plant-based alternatives.

## Vegan Kartoffelsalat

Kartoffelsalat is a popular German potato salad. A vegan version can be prepared without mayonnaise or eggs.

### Ingredients

- 1 kilogram potatoes
- 1 onion
- 100 milliliters vegetable broth
- 3 tablespoons vinegar
- 2 tablespoons mustard
- 4 tablespoons plant oil
- Salt and pepper
- Fresh herbs such as parsley or chives

### Preparation

Boil the potatoes until tender. Let them cool, peel them, and slice them. Mix vegetable broth, vinegar, mustard, oil, salt, and pepper into a dressing. Add chopped onion and herbs. Combine the potatoes with the dressing and let the salad rest before serving.

## German vegan alternatives

Bratwurst can be replaced with plant-based sausages. Schnitzel can be made from seitan, soy protein, oyster mushrooms, or breaded tofu. Cream-based sauces can be made with oat cream or cashew cream.
